So, what if your organization's not of the common-garden, plain-vanilla variety? Is Sales Process Engineering still applicable?

The answer is yes. And to prove the versatility of SPE, I've prepared a number of resources for you, exploring its implications for different environments.

What if we're a very small firm?

Because SPE explicitly recognizes that team members have finite capacity (and rations that limited capacity carefully) it's actually quite applicable to very small firms (or even start-ups). I've written a short paper that showcases the different resourcing models we apply in three situations (a large firm, a mid-sized firm and a start-up). You can download that paper here:

https://www.salesprocessengineering.net/wp-content/SPE-Three-Scenarios.pdf

What if we sell things that are very complex, or expensive?

There are two major challenges in complex-sales environments. The first is simplifying your engagement model. The second is ensuring that salespeople don't get entangled in delivery. You can read about how handle both here:

Simplify engagement model: http://tinyurl.com/26278xp

Disentangle salespeople from delivery: http://tinyurl.com/2fatmjh

What if we sell via resellers, agents or manufacturers' reps?

If you sell through an arms-length intermediary, our standard SPEC model is definitely not appropriate for your firm. But the basic principles still apply. To learn about our approach to channel management, read this article:

How to get the most out of representatives and distributors: http://tinyurl.com/29g74yb

What if we're selling a commodity product?

If you are selling a commodity product, you certainly don't want to over-engineer your sales function. All you'll do is drive-up costs and generate noise in the market! In this case, you want to be sure to use the basic SPE principles to drive-down your engagement cost. You should consider shifting your sales front-line to the telephone (inside sales) and using your field representatives to perform only those tasks that absolutely must be performed in the field. You can read more here:

Your field rep should not necessarily be your salesperson: http://tinyurl.com/24pg3s4
